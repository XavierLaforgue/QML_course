# QML_course
An introduction to quantum computing and quantum machine learning from scratch.

The goal is to take students from zero to understanding the key concepts, promises, and expectations one may have in quantum machine learning.

This course is aimed at students with no prior exposure to quantum. Basic linear algebra is assumed to be known, but the first lecture contains a walk through.

The course syllabus is split in three lecures, each of them done over a whole day. 

- Lecture 1: Basics of quantum computing: some maths, some debunking, some current status of the field
- Lecture 2: Standard quantum algorithms: quantum Fourier transform, Grover, Shor, variational quantum algorithms
- Lecture 3: Quantum machine learning: neural networks, kernel methods, quantum reservoir computing, QLSTM. Also, what to expect/no to expect from QML, compared with classical ML


This series of lecture is part of a broader programme on full-stack quantum computing from an applications & algorithms perspective.
